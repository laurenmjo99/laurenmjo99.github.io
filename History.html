The History of Coffee

First dates, interviews, work meetings, and many other situations begin with a simple "Should we meet for coffee?" With how relevant coffee consumption is in today's society, it's hard to imagine not waking up to a hot cup every morning. Where did coffee come from? Has it always been a social drink? Read on to learn more about the history of coffee!

Where Does Coffee Come from?

Coffee beans were first discovered by goat herder, Kaldi on the Ethiopian Plateau. Legend has it that he noticed that his goats grew energetic and restless whenever they ate the "berries" off a nearby tree. When he brought the beans to the local monastery, the abbot used the beans in a drink to keep him alert during prayer. Soon, word of "energizing beans" would spread across the world!

Coffee circulated around the world, and quickly became loved. In 1773, the colonists rebelled against King George III's hefty tax on tea. After the infamous Boston Tea Party, coffee became the number 1 American drink preference.

Whatt the turn of the 18th century, coffee was on of the most profitable trade and export crops in the world. Coffee helped to build up many economies which in turn established entire nations. Currently, coffee is the second most desired commodity in the world, right behind oil!

The rich flavor of coffee is accompanied by an even richer history. Coffee's effects on trade, economies, and societal norms have changed the world as we know it. Perhaps the event of Kaldi's goats discovering the coffee bean could be referred to as "the bean heard 'round the world!"
